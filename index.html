About Me

I'm a Computer Science undergraduate at Stanford University. Officially, I study theoretical and programming computer science, including algorithms, data structures, computer organization, and programming languages. But really? I work on what I love. I seek every opportunity I can to learn something new, build things I'm interested in, and engage in thought-provoking discussions.

Enjoyment

I took my first computer science class in Java in the spring of 2015, and I was immediately drawn by the creativity and logic it requires. Computer science also allows me to contribute significant value with my code to make a positive impact on many people. My other interests include cosmology, physics, and math, which I hope to combine with my love for computer science to develop valuable tools.

When I’m not doing school work, you can find me working on personal projects to improve and expand my current understanding of a topic. I am a part of the Stanford Solar Car Project’s code team for the 2019 cycle. We are currently working on fixing bugs in embedded code, cruise control, and real-time telemetry systems, as well as redesigning the telemetry system.

Some of my favorite things to do outside work include dancing for Stanford Bhangra (a Punjabi folk dance team on campus), playing acoustic and classical guitar, walking around campus on warm afternoons, and stargazing on clear nights. I love exploring new things. In fact, some of my most exciting experiences have happened when I tried something I had never done before.
